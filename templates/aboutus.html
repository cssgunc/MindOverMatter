Our Story

Mind Above Matter came together when the three of us took the first-year seminar of PLCY 89 in the fall of 2018. We, and our other teammate, Rachel Jones, came up with the idea for a system that would aggregate all of UNC's mental health resources in one place. Our team noticed that all of these resources had to be found separately, often taking up a lot of time and causing confusion for the user. After going through multiple entrepreneurship competitions and joining the CUBE 5.0 Cohort, we eventually decided on creating a website to solve this problem. Thanks to the spectacular work by the Spring 2020 Education Team of CS + Social Good, we were able to develop this website while also establishing ourselves as a non-profit organization.

Although our idea has evolved, we still stand by the same mission statement we came up with in the beginning: Our mission is to make practical mental health services accessible to UNC students. We aspire that one day mental health will be as easy to talk about and seek treatment for as the common cold, so it is seen as equivalent to physical wellbeing. We’re hoping to foster a local community that ends the stigma surrounding mental illness and encourages college students to reach out for assistance with problems with their mental health. Here at UNC, we hope to reform the current scatter of mental health organizations to create a more practical and user-friendly system of obtaining help and information for mental illness.
